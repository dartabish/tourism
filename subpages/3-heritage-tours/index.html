Kashmir Heritage Tour

Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir

About this tour

Nestled in the heart of the Kashmir Valley, Srinagar is a city where history resonates on every street corner, ancient architecture stands as a testament to a vibrant past, and the traditions of centuries are preserved against the backdrop of the majestic Himalayas.

Join us on this unforgettable voyage into the heart of Srinagar, where every stone, every street, and every vista tells a story of enduring heritage and timeless beauty. Discover the soul of Kashmir, its people, its crafts, and its celebrated traditions, on a journey that will leave you with memories to cherish for a lifetime.

Highlights

  • Indulge in Wazwan, a symphony of flavors that define Kashmiri culinary artistry.
  • Witness the magic of Kashmiri artisans, sculpting heritage with their hands.
  • Feel the warmth of exquisite Pashmina
  • Warm your soul with a cup of aromatic Kashmiri Kahwa, spiced with saffron and almonds.

    • Itinerary

    A vibrant marketplace in the old city, offering traditional Kashmiri handicrafts, spices, and textiles amidst intricate wooden architecture and narrow lanes, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of Kashmir.

    A revered mosque and Sufi shrine built in honor of Mir Sayyid Ali Hamdani, notable for its striking wooden architecture and serene atmosphere, symbolizing the spiritual heritage of Srinagar.

    A historical monument known for its Persian architectural influences and the blend of Islamic and Hindu elements, commemorating the 15th-century ruler Zain-ul-Abidin's contributions to Kashmir's cultural landscape.

    A unique stone mosque built by Mughal Empress Noor Jahan, distinguished by its nine arches and extensive use of stone, showcasing the Mughal influence on Kashmiri architecture.

    A sacred Hindu shrine on Hari Parbat hill, dedicated to goddess Sharika, offering panoramic views of Srinagar and serving as a spiritual center for the Kashmiri Pandit community.

    One of Kashmir's largest mosques, featuring a vast courtyard and wooden colonnades, representing the zenith of Indo-Saracenic architecture and the rich Islamic tradition of the region.

    An 18th-century fort with panoramic views of Srinagar, reflecting the historical narrative of Mughal, Sikh, and Durrani rule, and serving as a testament to the region's strategic importance.

    A significant Sikh Gurudwara marking the visit of Guru Hargobind Singh, known for its peaceful environment and spiritual significance to the Sikh community in Kashmir.
